[1]
Teitelbaum, J.
: This is an application by the plaintiff, Frantz Etienne, pursuant to s. 77 of the
Official Languages Act
, R.S.C. 1985, c. 0-3.01 (
Act
).
"77(1) Any person who has made a complaint to the Commissioner in respect of a right or duty under ss. 4 to 7, ss. 10 to 13 or Part IV or V, or in respect of s. 91, may apply to the court for a remedy under this Part.
"77(2) An application may be made under subs. (1) within 60 days after
(a) the results of an investigation of the complaint by the Commissioner are reported to the complainant under subs. 64(1),
(b) the complainant is informed of the recommendations of the Commissioner under subs. 64(2),
or
(c) the complainant is informed of the Commissioner's decision to refuse or cease to investigate the complaint under subs. 58(5),
or within such further time as the court may, either before or after the expiration of those 60 days, fix or allow.
"77(3) Where a complaint is made to the Commissioner under this Act but the complainant is not informed of the results of the investigation of the complaint under subs. 64(1), of the recommendations of the Commissioner under subs. 58(5) within six months after the complaint is made, the complainant may make an application under subs. (1) at any time thereafter.
"77(4) Where, in proceedings under subs. (1), the court concludes that a federal institution has failed to comply with this Act, the court may grant such remedy as it considers appropriate and just in the circumstances.
"77(5) Nothing in this section abrogates or derogates from any right of action a person might have other than the right of action set out in this section."
[2]
On June 21, 1991, the plaintiff filed into the court registry in Montreal an Avis de Requête in which he requests, among other requests:
"La requête vise aussi l'obtention d'une prolongation du délai normal pour présenter un recours à cette honorable Cour au titre de l'article 77(2) de la
Loi
."
[3]
The plaintiff, at this time, was representing himself. According to this application, the respondent is Le Ministre de la Défense nationale, with Le Commissaire aux langues officielles du Canada and Le Commissaire aux droits de la personne du Canada as mis-en-cause.
[4]
On July 5, 1991, La Commission canadienne des droits de la personne filed a notice of application requesting:
"La requête vise à faire mettre la mise en cause hors de cause dans l'instance déposée devant cette Cour par Frantz Etienne le 21 juin 1991. La requête est fondée sur le motif qu'aucune conclusion n'est demandée, ni ne pourrait être demandée contre la mise en cause, la Commission canadienne des droits de la personne."
[5]
It is interesting to note that La Commission canadienne des droits de la personne is not named as a mis-en-cause in the plaintiff's original notice of application.
[6]
On November 29, 1991, the plaintiff desisted as against Le Commissaire aux droits de la personne du Canada. On March 2, 1992, he desisted as against Le Commissaire aux langues officielles du Canada.
[7]
The plaintiff now is represented by an attorney. On March 27, 1992, the plaintiff filed an Avis de Requête Amendée whereby he names Sa Majesté la Reine and the Ministre de la Défense nationale as defendants wherein he asks for:
"I. Une injonction par le biais de l'article 77 sous paragraphe 4 de la
Loi sur les langues officielles
contre les défendeurs leur ordonnant selon les articles 35, 36 et 39 de la
Loi sur les langues officielles
de fournir et dispenser dans la langue française les cours de perfectionnement auxquels le demandeur a fait ou fera une demande d'admission de même que lui fournir toute documentation relative auxdits cours dans la langue officielle de son choix, soit le français, et ordonner aux défendeurs de revenir devant cette Cour à chaque année afin de démontrer que les cours auxquels le demandeur aurait droit d'accès sont effectivement dispensés en français et que leur documentation afférente est disponible en français.
"II. L'obtention de dommages contre la défenderesse correspondant à une perte de salaire pour un total de 7 307,72 $ en vertu de l'article 77 sous paragraphe 4 de la
Loi sur les langues officielles
.
"III. L'obtention de dommages non-pécuniaires contre la défenderesse, pour la somme de 30 000 $ en vertu de l'article 77 sous paragraph 4 de la
Loi sur les langues officielles
pour compenser les humiliations et inconvéniens subis par le demandeur.
"IV. L'obtention de dommages exemplaires contre la défenderesse, pour la somme de 50 000 $ en vertu de l'article 77 sous paragraphe 4 de la
Loi sur les langues officielles
pour les actes arbitraires commis par les préposés du Ministère de la défense nationale, afin de décourager une telle conduite dans l'avenir et de voir à l'application réelle de la
Loi sur les langues officielles
.
"V. Tout autre recours ou ordonnance que cette Honorable Cour jugera bon d'accorder;"
[8]
The plaintiff then filed a number of notices of application for various reasons which were decided by Mr. Justice Joyal or continued sine die.
[9]
On April 22, 1992, the plaintiff filed the present application in which he states:
"Prenez Avis que la présente requête du requérant, Frantz Etienne, est présentée afin de prolonger les délais de dépôt des recours. Cette requête sera présentée à cette honorable Cour siégeant dans la Ville de Montréal, le 4 mai 1992 à 10:00 a.m. au Palais de Justice de Montréal, 1 rue Notre-Dame Est, 11e étage.
"La requête est fondée sur le motif que le requérant, qui agissait sans procureurs à l'époque, a cru qu'il avait déposé son avis de requête en vertu de l'article 77(1) à l'intérieur des délais légaux."
[10]
From the affidavit of the plaintiff filed on April 22, 1992 with this present notice of application, we know the following:
"1. Je suis le demandeur dans l'action ci-haut mentionnée;
"2. J'ai fait une plainte à la Commission des langues officielles en date du 21 février 1990, tel qu'indiqué par le formulaire de réception de ladite commission, ci-joint comme Pièce R-1 à mon affidavit;
"3. J'ai reçu du Commissariat aux langues officielles un récépissé de ma plainte, par lettre en date du 1er mars 1990, ci-joint comme Pièce R-2 à mon affidavit;
"4. J'ai reçu avis des résultats de l'enquête du commissariat aux langues officielles par lettre en date du 14 mars 1991 et reçue le ou vers le 23 mars 1991, copie ci-jointe comme Pièce R-3 à mon affidavit;
"5. Dès que j'ai reçu les résultats de l'enquête du Commissariat aux langues officielles, j'ai pris la décision de faire valoir mes droits devant la Cour;
"6. J'ai moi-même préparé les procédures juridiques dans ce dossier, sans consulter un avocat;
"7. Je pensais que le délai pour intenter les procédures ne courait que durant les jours ouvrables et que ce délai expirait le 21 juin 1991."
[11]
From this affidavit, and I have no other evidence before me, the reason why the plaintiff did not proceed within the delay stipulated in s. 77 is that he thought that the delays to commence proceedings pursuant to s. 77 only ran "durant les jours ouvrables et que ce délai expirait le 21 juin 1991".
[12]

(Exhibit R-3 to plaintiff's affidavit)
[13]
Assuming the plaintiff received the above letter on March 23, 1991, as he states in s. 4 of his affidavit, not before this date although this is also possible as there is no ordinary mail delivery on a Saturday as March 23, 1991 is and there is nothing on the March 14, 1991 letter to indicate it was sent other than by ordinary mail and pursuant to the last paragraph of the March 14, 1991 letter and pursuant to s. 77, the delay to commence proceedings would be the 22nd of May 1991. If we were to count, as the plaintiff states in his affidavit "les jours ouvrables", the delay to commence proceedings would be June 14, 1991, some seven days before the date June 21, 1991, when the plaintiff commenced proceedings.
[14]
It must be noted, and here I repeat what I have stated, the letter of March 14, 1991 clearly instructs the plaintiff that he has 60 days in which to commence proceedings from the date he receives the letter dated March 14, 1991.
"Veuillez toutefois noter que le délai de soixante jours prévue par la
Loi
court à partir de la date à laquelle vous recevrez cette lettre."
[15]
The issue is to decide what meaning is to be ascribed to the words contained in s. 77 of the
Act
. Is the court, for no matter what reason, or for that matter even when no reason is given, to simply extend the delays in which to allow a person to commence proceedings? I think not. What would be the purpose of stipulating a delay in the statute if for any reason or for no reason the court extends the delays in which to commence legal proceedings?
[16]
The court has been given the discretion to extend the delay to commence proceedings but the court can only exercise this discretion in a judicious manner, that is to say, if the plaintiff submits an acceptable reason. I say an acceptable reason to indicate that the court should attempt not to deprive one of a judicial right because of delay but one must have some valid reason for failing to commence legal proceedings within the legal delays stipulated in a statute.
[17]
Counsel for the plaintiff states that the plaintiff is not an attorney (and thus should be forgiven), that the defendant will suffer no prejudice, that justice would better be served if an extension were allowed and that the issue of the action is such that the action should be allowed to proceed.
[18]
Counsel for the defendants informed the court that she does not consent to the granting of the application but does not object. She states she leaves the matter to the court.
[19]
It is true the plaintiff is not an attorney but it is also true that the plaintiff is a well learned individual who would and should have clearly understood the paragraph in the March 14, 1991 letter telling him he had 60 days to commence proceedings from the date he received the letter. If he did not understand what the paragraph meant he should have consulted an attorney. Furthermore, the plaintiff's affidavit said he thought it meant "jours ouvrables". Assuming this to be correct, he is still outside his delays.
[20]
The plaintiff states that there is no prejudice to the defendants. I do not agree. The defendants, if I grant the present application is prejudiced because the defendants would have to defend an action at considerable cost. It is simply to say "the defendants would not be prejudiced by allowing the application" when one is not the defendant.
[21]
Would justice be better served by allowing the present application rather than refusing it? I am satisfied that justice would better be served by not allowing the present application for the reason that some meaning
must
be given to the section in the
Act
. To allow the present application on the simple pretense of not understanding the statement "Veuillez toutefois noter que le délai de soixante jours prévue par la
Loi
court à partir de la date à laquelle vous recevrez cette lettre" would be to make s. 77(2) of the
Act
meaningless.
[22]
I cannot but dismiss the present application with costs.
Application dismissed.
